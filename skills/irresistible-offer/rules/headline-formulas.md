# 20 Proven Headline Formulas

Headlines are the single highest-leverage element in any ad. 80% of people read the headline; only 20% read the body. These 20 formulas are battle-tested across thousands of campaigns.

## Rules Before Writing

- **40 characters max** for Meta Ads headlines (truncated on mobile after ~40)
- **Lead with the benefit**, not the feature
- **Specificity wins**: "Save 14 hours/week" beats "Save time"
- **Numbers outperform words**: "3 Steps" beats "A Few Steps"
- **Test 5 headlines minimum** per ad set via Advantage+ Creative

## The 20 Formulas

### 1. How to [Desired Outcome]
The classic. Works because it promises a transformation with an implied roadmap.
- "How to Land Your First Dev Job"
- "Como lanzar tu startup en 90 dias"

### 2. [Number] Ways to [Desired Outcome]
Listicles work in ads too. The number sets expectations and creates curiosity.
- "7 Ways to Double Your Freelance Income"
- "5 errores que matan tu productividad"

### 3. The Secret to [Desired Outcome]
Implies insider knowledge. Use when your method is genuinely differentiated.
- "The Secret to Passing Technical Interviews"
- "El secreto de los founders que recaudan capital"

### 4. [Do Desired Thing] Without [Pain/Sacrifice]
Attacks the effort/sacrifice variable in the value equation.
- "Learn to Code Without Quitting Your Job"
- "Escala tu negocio sin quemar tu equipo"

### 5. Warning: [Consequence of Not Acting]
Fear of loss is 2x more motivating than potential gain.
- "Warning: Your Skills Are Already Outdated"
- "Alerta: Tu competencia ya esta usando IA"

### 6. [Desired Outcome] in [Specific Timeframe]
Attacks the time delay variable. Be honest about the timeframe.
- "Ship Your MVP in One Weekend"
- "Tu primer cliente en 30 dias"

### 7. Are You Making These [Number] Mistakes?
Curiosity + fear of being wrong. People click to check themselves.
- "Are You Making These 5 Resume Mistakes?"
- "Cometes estos 3 errores al presentar tu pitch?"

### 8. [Famous Person/Company] [Does/Uses/Recommends] This
Authority transfer. Only use real endorsements or well-known references.
- "Y Combinator Teaches This Framework"
- "Lo que Netflix aprendio sobre cultura de equipo"

### 9. Here's What [Successful People] Do Differently
Aspirational modeling. Works especially well in LATAM markets.
- "Here's What Top 1% Engineers Do Differently"
- "Esto es lo que hacen los founders exitosos"

### 10. Stop [Bad Habit] -- Start [Good Alternative]
Direct command format. Creates an immediate contrast.
- "Stop Applying Blind -- Start Networking Smart"
- "Deja de improvisar, empieza a sistematizar"

### 11. [Objection]? Here's the Truth.
Calls out the objection directly, then promises to resolve it.
- "Too Old to Learn to Code? Here's the Truth."
- "No tienes experiencia? Esto es lo que importa."

### 12. Why [Surprising/Counterintuitive Claim]
Pattern interrupt through a claim that challenges assumptions.
- "Why Your Portfolio Is Hurting Your Job Search"
- "Por que estudiar mas no te hace mejor programador"

### 13. [Specific Result] Guaranteed (or [Penalty])
Combines outcome specificity with risk reversal. Highest-converting formula.
- "3 Job Interviews in 90 Days, Guaranteed"
- "Tu primer ingreso online en 60 dias o te devolvemos todo"

### 14. What [Group] Knows About [Topic] That You Don't
Creates an information gap. Works for B2B and professional audiences.
- "What Senior Engineers Know About System Design"
- "Lo que los VCs buscan y nadie te dice"

### 15. [Impressive Stat]: How They Did It
Social proof + curiosity about the method.
- "2,400 Graduates Hired: Here's How"
- "87% de nuestros alumnos consiguen trabajo en 6 meses"

### 16. The [Adjective] Guide to [Desired Outcome]
Implies comprehensiveness or ease. Pick the right adjective.
- "The Complete Guide to Freelancing in Tech"
- "La guia definitiva para tu primer SaaS"

### 17. [Audience], This Is For You
Direct address creates instant relevance. Reader self-selects.
- "Junior Developers, This Is For You"
- "Emprendedores de LATAM: esto es para ti"

### 18. Finally: [Something They've Been Waiting For]
Implies the reader has been searching. Good for novel solutions.
- "Finally: A Bootcamp That Actually Gets You Hired"
- "Por fin: un programa que si funciona"

### 19. [Simple Number] Steps to [Desired Outcome]
Simplicity sells. Low perceived effort.
- "3 Steps to Your First Paying Client"
- "4 pasos para tu primer deploy en produccion"

### 20. What Happens When [Trigger Event]
Story-driven curiosity. Implies a transformation narrative.
- "What Happens When a Teacher Learns to Code"
- "Que pasa cuando un ingeniero lanza su propia startup"

## Headline Testing Cheat Sheet

When writing 5 headlines for Advantage+ Creative, pick 5 different formulas from this list. Never use 5 variations of the same formula -- that tests execution, not angle.

**Good test set (diverse formulas):**
1. How to Land a Remote Dev Job (formula 1)
2. 3 Steps to Your First Interview (formula 19)
3. 2,400 Graduates Hired Last Year (formula 15)
4. Too Old to Learn to Code? (formula 11)
5. Ship Your MVP This Weekend (formula 6)

**Bad test set (same formula repeated):**
1. How to Get Hired as a Developer
2. How to Pass Technical Interviews
3. How to Build a Winning Portfolio
4. How to Network in Tech
5. How to Negotiate Your Salary
